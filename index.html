Haematology Tests

We provide comprehensive diagnostic services for blood disorders starting from the evaluation of simple anaemias going across complex multifactorial diseases including bleeding and thrombotic disorders extending to malignancies such as acute leukaemia. Services include:

  • Routine Haematology
  • Automated and advanced Immunohematology
  • Complete bone marrow evaluation with Immunohistochemistry
  • Advanced Coagulation assays for bleeding and thrombotic disorders
  • Multiparametric Flowcytometry for Haematolymphoid malignancies, Immune disorders, PNH & Stem cell enumeration
  • High end advanced testing for detection of Haemoglobinopathies
  • Automated Urinalysis with advanced imaging microscopy
  • Automated ESR & Body fluid analysis

Genetics Tests

We provide comprehensive tests for the detection of chromosomal abnormalities, such as pre-natal & post-natal constitutional disorders, oncology diseases including blood cancers & other organ cancers. For patients with hematologic or oncologic malignancies, cytogenetic testing plays a key role in the diagnosis, prognosis, and selection and monitoring of treatment. Services include:

  • Karyotyping - Chromosome analysis for prenatal samples (amniotic fluid, chorionic villous tissue, products of conception), peripheral blood, bone marrow, lymphomas and solid tumours
  • FISH (fluorescence in situ hybridization) assays

Cytology

Aside from tissue biopsy, cytology serves as an indispensable tool in screening and diagnosing cancer.   • Gynaecological Cytology
  • Non-Gynaecological Cytology
  • Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology
  • BAL Differential Cell Count

Genetics Tests

Genes are DNA instructions that we all inherit from our parents. Different “mistakes” in our genes, called mutations, can cause different diseases. Some of these diseases are rare, like cystic fibrosis or Huntington’s disease, others are common, like cancer. Genetic tests can be used to confirm the diagnosis or give you information about possible development of a disease.

  • PCR
  • DNA Sequencing
  • Cytogenetics (Karyotyping and FISH)
  • Microarrays
